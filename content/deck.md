# Monument

**The partnership is being asked to commit $20M to a 50-year asset.**

**What is that asset supposed to be?**

---

## How to read this deck

- Visual summary of the analytical memo. The memo carries the full argument.
- Sits alongside the engine and the partner-facing simulator in the hub.
- Does not recommend a scenario. Each scenario is presented with its strongest case.
- Read in whatever order is useful. The memo is linear; this deck is not.

## Great starting point

- **The team is real.** Hart Howerton, Faure Halverson, Forge and Finish, Dentons, Greenberg Traurig and Hale Wood, Kirton McConkie, with Will Munger on wildlife and range.
- **The design is genuinely better than YC's original.** 92 households on 15,000 acres versus YC's 750 on 15,200. 80 percent conservation versus YC's 20 percent. Dispersed nodes, not a centralized lodge complex.
- **The Foundation is structural innovation.** Private Operating Foundation, not a syndicated conservation easement. Active charitable purpose. Tier-one tax counsel.
- **The Powder Haven adjacency is structural.** Reed Hastings's institutional underwriting of the ski amenity for the full economic life of this asset.

> It is acknowledged that not all of these lots may survive the final planning due to potential issues like water constraints or conflicts with hunting and wildlife concerns.

The team is telling us the substrate matters. The memo proceeds from that shared starting point.

## The frame

**Three horizons.** The deal asks the partnership to commit at all three:

- New equity: 6 to 10 years (real estate hold)
- Foundation: indefinite, perpetual yield
- Land: 50 years, multigenerational

**The property is substrate, not real estate.** 15,000 acres at the top of the Weber and Bear drainages. Wintering range for an elk herd the state manages at population objective. Adjacent to North Fork Park's International Dark Sky designation. Whatever happens here cascades downhill, across fence lines, and upward into the sky. The deal is one drainage's footprint on a regional substrate.

## Nine gaps

Each gap below names a place where the team's narrative and the team's cash flow do not yet match.

1. The cash flow monetizes 27 lots, not 50.
2. Carrying capacity at the proposed activity scale.
3. Cache County FR-40 zoning. Rezone required, referendum risk.
4. Foundation sized at $30-40M, not the $80M regional framing implies.
5. POF determination pending. Investor capital before it clears.
6. Four pillars compete at 42 ranches.
7. Water cost is not in the cash flow.
8. Operating undercapitalization. Same shape as Whisper Ridge.
9. Most of the equity commitment is not financing construction.

---

## Gap 1: The cash flow monetizes 27 lots, not 50

- The master plan has 50 lots across Monument 1, 2, 3, 4.
- The deck describes 42 ranches plus 50 social members.
- 15 ranches are marked "Firm" at $0 in the pro forma: already sold, no marginal revenue.
- 8 placeholder rows sit at $0: deferred or unpriced.
- 27 lots are revenue-producing in the cash flow: 10 Phase 1 at $5,000K, 12 Phase 2 at $5,500K, 5 Phase 3 at $6,000K.
- Total revenue $146M. Foundation contribution $32.4M (Phase 1 lots at $1.2M each).

The narrative implies all 50 lots monetize. The cash flow only contemplates 27.

---

## Gap 2: Carrying capacity at the proposed activity scale

- **Snowpack:** down 16 percent since 1979 (USU Janet Quinney Lawson Institute).
- **Wildlife:** mule deer at 73 percent of state objective. Elk culled on northern Utah CWMUs in 2024 for "long-term habitat damage from overgrazing" (UDWR wildlife board).
- **Roads:** 1.5 to 2.4 mi/sq mi estimated; the threshold for sustaining ungulate populations is 1 (TRCP / CPW 2022).
- **Light:** 170 to 320K cumulative lumens versus ~50-100K for a small rural town's main street.

The team's April 30 admission is the strongest internal evidence that the substrate question is real.

---

## Gap 4: Foundation sizing

- Operating cost peaks $2.4-2.6M/yr by 2032.
- Yield at 5-7% on $30-40M principal sits at $1.5-2.8M. Covers operations narrowly.
- Grants assumed $500K/yr at steady state, $4.9M total over the horizon.
- Foundation end balance 2037: **$33.5M**, almost entirely from $32.4M of lot contributions.
- Yield is consumed by expenses; the principal does not grow.

**A $30-40M Foundation administers Monument's stewardship. Regional-scale ecological work is closer to $80-100M.**

---

## Gap 5: Tax timing and POF determination

- The $3:$1 leverage depends on POF determination, non-§170(h) structure, HBU defensibility, and donative intent.
- Four 2025 Tax Court cases rejected the DCF yield-plan as "fantasy economics": Beaverdam Creek (T.C. Memo 2025-53), Ranch Springs (164 T.C. No. 6), Jackson Stone South (T.C. Memo 2025-96), Excelsior Aggregates.
- In Beaverdam Creek, a $22M deduction was reduced to under $200K.
- OBBBA 2026 cuts roughly 10 percent: effective leverage closer to $2.70-$2.80.
- POF determination is pending. The deal contemplates investor capital before it clears.

---

## Gap 9: Most of the equity commitment is not financing construction

- **$20M committed.** The full ask from the partnership.
- **~$10M peak deployment.** The maximum equity ever drawn at one time.
- **~$8.9M Peak Funding Requirement.** The construction trough the equity actually covers.

Roughly **$11M of committed capital is not financing construction.**

On the team's own cash flow, the pre-existing debt retires from sales proceeds with $8.7M to spare. So the rationale that equity is needed to retire the 2032 balloon is not what the cash flow shows.

The $11M likely covers some combination of operating gap, GP true-up, distribution flexibility, and execution-risk buffer. The team has not yet itemized which.

The GP fee stream is calculated on committed capital, not deployed capital. The larger the raise, the larger the fee stream, independent of whether the capital is deployed.

---

## Four more gaps

**Gap 3: FR-40 zoning requires rezone.** Year-round residence is prohibited under Cache County's FR-40. Rezone to Resort Recreation required. Powder Mountain just did the same (Feb 2025, 4-3 vote). Wasatch Peaks precedent: 4 years of litigation, settled January 2024.

**Gap 6: Four pillars compete at scale.** Ski/heli Nov-Apr, hunt Sep-Nov, ranch year-round. No season undisturbed. At 42 ranches with full mech rec the pillars compete; at 25 lots with light mech rec they coexist.

**Gap 7: Water cost is not in the cash flow.** 42 ranches plus lodge nodes plus 4,000 sheep on a property straddling two water rights areas. If 8-12 lots fall out on water constraints, $146M revenue compresses to roughly $80-105M.

**Gap 8: Operating undercapitalization risk.** $17.5M total dues versus $43M club operating expenses over the horizon. Whisper Ridge failed the same shape. If sales slip, the operating model comes under pressure first.

## The tax-benefit dimension is non-monotonic

| Scenario | Per-lot | Lots | Leverage | Realization | **Realized** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Base | $1.2M | 27 | × $3 | × 1.0 | **$97M** |
| Staged | $1.4M | 27 | × $3 | × 0.8 | **$90.7M** |
| Compact | $1.8M | 32 | × $3 | × 0.8 | **$138M** |
| Conservation | $3.2M | 25 | × $3 | × 0.8 | **$192M** |

The 0.8 realization rate is a haircut for OBBBA, audit risk, and appraisal challenge.

- **Staged: haircut wins.** A modest per-lot increase ($1.2M to $1.4M) fails to overcome the 0.8 haircut. Realized benefit falls below Base.
- **Compact and Conservation: scaling wins.** Larger per-lot contributions dominate the haircut. Conservation captures nearly twice Base in absolute tax dollars.

More Foundation contribution does not always mean more realized tax benefit. The intuition that conservation-leaning scenarios sacrifice tax benefit is wrong on the absolute-dollar dimension.

## The engine: what flexes, what doesn't

A parametric engine built for this analysis, separate from the team's pro forma. Nine sheets in calculation order. Base reconciles to the team's six headline numbers.

**Reliability gradient across the four scenarios:**

- **Base.** Reconciles to the team's pro forma. Numbers are the audit trail.
- **Staged.** Same lot count and member base as Base. Numbers rest on the same reconciled basis.
- **Compact and Conservation.** Lot count and revenue flex correctly. Precise XIRR and MOIC deferred to Monte Carlo, where club opex and smaller items will be flexed. Directional uplift over Base is real and large.

**Flexes with scenario:** lot count, lot pricing, Foundation contribution and terms, development scope, amenity scope, cabin cost, tax realization rate.

**Does not yet flex:** club operating costs (held at Base program across all scenarios), cabin program detail (coarse multiplier, not full model).

## Plan As Proposed (Base Case)

**Optimizes for:** maximum financial return, 6-10 year hold, full tax shelter.

- 27 lots, $146M revenue, $32.4M Foundation contribution
- Levered XIRR **16.2%**, MOIC **1.79x**, Net Profit **$7.9M**
- Foundation end balance **$33.5M**
- Realized tax benefit **$97M** (full $3:$1, 1.0 realization)
- Peak Funding Requirement **$8.9M**

**Risks accepted:** POF determination pending; HBU vulnerable under 2025 Tax Court precedents; OBBBA 2026 cuts; FR-40 entitlement unresolved; substrate strain at proposed scale; lot pricing is the single most sensitive input.

**Asks:** $20M new equity at existing terms, 80% common dilution.

---

## Plan With Rigor (Staged)

**Optimizes for:** committing equity only after procedural risks resolve. Buyer pays for the rigor through a 10% price premium.

The first version of this scenario treated rigor as an overlay. It did not work; the cost ate the return. Plan With Rigor is built as a different product: same 27 lots, premium pricing, structural protections.

- Phase 1 / 2 / 3 lot prices **$5,500K / $6,000K / $6,500K**
- Foundation contribution per lot **$1.4M**
- POF determination and tax counsel opinion as closing conditions
- Investor capital in escrow until milestones clear
- Zoning rezone secured before pricing committed
- 0.8 tax realization rate modeled as base

**Engine outputs:** revenue $159.5M; Foundation end balance **$35.4M**; tax benefit **$90.7M**; PFR $8.9M.
**Levered XIRR 29.1%, MOIC 2.57x.** Same reconciled basis as Base.

At $90.7M, realized tax is slightly below Base's $97M. The haircut more than offsets the higher per-lot contribution. Staged's case is the return and the de-risking, not the tax benefit.

**Asks:** $20M into escrow, milestone-tied drawdown, ~12-month timeline impact.

---

## Reduced Footprint (Compact)

**Optimizes for:** delivering to children and grandchildren the experience that brought the partnership here, through a structure that pays for itself.

- **32 lots**, single phase. No Phase 2/3 entitlement layer.
- $6.5M average lot price
- Foundation contribution per lot **$1.8M**
- Reduced mech rec scope (0.6x multiplier on heli, ski-pass, snowcat lines)
- Single rezone, smaller political surface

**Engine outputs:** revenue $208M; Foundation end balance **$68.0M**; tax benefit **$138M**; PFR $7.8M.

Investor returns run well above Base. Precise XIRR and MOIC are deferred to the Monte Carlo stage, where club opex and other smaller items will be flexed against the smaller member base.

**Risks:** lot pricing assumption still binds; legacy debt refinances at 7.5% in 2032 if sales-funded payoff slips.

**Asks:** $20M new equity, single-phase commitment, lighter operating footprint.

---

## Conservation First (Conservation)

**Optimizes for:** great-grandchildren hunting the same elk herd. Monument as a multigenerational ecological asset rather than a multigenerational real estate asset.

- **25 lots** at $7.5M premium average
- Foundation contribution per lot **$3.2M**
- Foundation target $80M with structural surplus
- Foundation yield modeled conservatively at 5%
- Regional contribution mandate **$1M/yr** (USU partnerships, watershed work, dark-sky preservation, civic engagement)
- Mech rec light; slower entitlement; longer hold

**Engine outputs:** revenue $187.5M; Foundation end balance **$82.2M**; tax benefit **$192M**; PFR $6.6M.

Realized tax benefit at $192M is nearly twice Base's $97M in absolute dollars. Investor returns run above Base on a longer hold; precise XIRR and MOIC are deferred to Monte Carlo.

**Risks:** smaller cash-on-cash; longer hold (10+ years); higher dues and IFs; a regional commitment the partnership honors in practice, not on a slide.

**Asks:** $20M new equity, 10+ year hold, higher dues, higher IFs, regional commitment.

## Custom scenario

A fifth column in the engine's Inputs sheet, user-editable.

- Defaults to Base Case values
- Yellow cells indicate inputs that can be changed
- The dropdown sets the active scenario to Custom; the engine recalculates fully downstream

**Useful for:**

- Stress-testing a single variable
- Building hybrid positions between named scenarios
- Exploring what happens if Foundation contribution lands between Base and Staged, or if mech rec scope drops without changing lot count

The Custom column is the partnership's working draft of any agreement that does not match one of the four named scenarios exactly.

## The Monte Carlo headline

Scenario analysis tells us how each design performs at its central case. Monte Carlo tells us how each performs across plausible variation in the inputs the deal does not control.

10,000 iterations per scenario. Five stochastic drivers (snowpack, sales velocity, tax realization, entitlement delay, Foundation yield). Two-tier criteria: survival floor and partnership objectives.

| Scenario | Survival | Objective | Median XIRR | Median MOIC |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Plan As Proposed (Base) | 0.74% | 0.00% | -2.91% | 0.67x |
| Plan With Rigor (Staged) | 12.40% | 0.00% | 8.22% | 1.33x |
| Reduced Footprint (Compact) | 34.43% | 3.25% | 61.71% | 5.06x |
| Conservation First | 63.35% | 47.02% | 44.70% | 3.65x |

**Conservation First is the strongest scenario by survival rate and by full-objective achievement.** Even Conservation achieves all six partnership objectives only about 47 percent of the time. That is the right number to plan around.

---

## What kills each scenario when it dies

Three stochastic drivers do most of the failure work across the four scenarios.

- **Snowpack drives scope and IRR collapse.** Correlation -0.69 to -0.78. When snowpack underperforms the historical 0.84 mean, lot pricing softens and modeled returns compress.

- **Foundation yield drives Foundation-target miss.** Correlation -0.50 to -0.71. The historical 6 percent mean sits below the team's 7 percent assumption; that gap flows directly to Foundation end balance.

- **Entitlement delay drives debt retirement and dues coverage failure together.** Correlation +0.55 to +0.79. The two criteria fail in the same iterations: long entitlement pushes sales later, peak funding need balloons, membership ramp lags.

This is the Whisper Ridge failure mode from Gap 8, quantified across the input distribution. Not a tail risk; a meaningful slice (9% Plan As Proposed, 7% Plan With Rigor, 2% Reduced Footprint, 14% Conservation).

---

## Structural protections that improve Conservation

Two refinements would move Conservation from 47 percent objective achievement toward better odds.

- **Closing-condition protections on entitlement timeline.** Roughly 14 percent of Conservation iterations fail debt retirement and dues coverage simultaneously, predominantly because of long entitlement delay. Requiring entitlement clearance as a closing condition (the way Plan With Rigor handles POF determination) removes that failure mode from the iterations the equity bears.

- **Foundation yield floor or larger per-lot contribution.** Roughly 50 percent of Conservation iterations fall short of the $80M Foundation objective bar because realized yield comes in below the 6 percent historical mean. Two options: a yield floor mechanism (deal underwrites top-ups if realized yield underperforms a threshold), or sizing the per-lot contribution to land deterministically above $80M with margin.

Even with these, the partnership still has to choose. The analysis does not make the decision for them.

---

## The pattern across scenarios

Reading the four scenarios as a set rather than as alternatives surfaces a structural fact:

**The team's strongest defense, for every gap, gets stronger at smaller scale and weaker at larger scale.**

- 25 lots + $80M Foundation: the deal survives every steel-man critique
- 42 ranches + $30M Foundation: real risk in every dimension
- Plan With Rigor sits in between, accepting the proposed lot ambition while de-risking the structural exposures

The gaps are a system, not a list. The system tightens or loosens with scale.

## What the partnership is deciding

Four scenarios. Four answers to four different questions about what the partnership wants the deal to optimize for. Each is internally coherent. Each is defensible. Each asks the partnership for something different.

The Monte Carlo points toward Conservation First as the strongest scenario by survival rate and objective achievement, and surfaces the structural refinements that would improve any chosen scenario.

This deck and the memo are meant to give the partnership the analytical scaffolding to come to its own answer.

**The question we are deciding is what we want this place to be in fifty years. Everything else is downstream of that.**
