## What this is

I spent time with the deck, the pro forma, and the meeting notes for the proposed $20M equity round. To test the four scenarios more rigorously than I could otherwise, I built a parametric financial engine and ran a Monte Carlo simulation against it. This hub presents what I found.

The intent is to support the partnership's diligence on the commitment, not to redirect the team's work. The team is strong, the master plan is materially better than comparable resorts at the same scale, and the Foundation as a Private Operating Foundation is creative structural engineering. The transparency the team has shown in the deck, the meeting notes, and our conversations made this analysis possible.

## How they perform

| Scenario | Revenue ($M) | Foundation end ($M) | Realized tax ($M) | Median IRR | Survival | Full objective |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Base | 146.0 | 33.3 | 97 | -2.9% | 0.7% | 0.0% |
| Staged | 159.5 | 35.4 | 91 | 8.2% | 12.4% | 0.0% |
| Compact | 208.0 | 68.0 | 138 | 61.7% | 34.4% | 3.3% |
| Conservation | 187.5 | 82.2 | 192 | 44.7% | 63.4% | 47.0% |

Revenue, Foundation end, and Realized tax are deterministic outputs from the engine at each scenario's central inputs. Median IRR, Survival rate, and Full objective are from a Monte Carlo of 10,000 iterations per scenario against five stochastic drivers (snowpack, sales velocity, tax realization, entitlement delay, Foundation yield) with documented correlation structure. Median IRR is the central tendency under that stress; the team's deterministic Base IRR at neutral inputs is 16.2 percent.

Survival means the deal stays viable on the partnership's basic terms (12% IRR floor, scenario-specific Foundation target, debt retired, scope maintained). Full objective means the deal meets all six stated partnership objectives, including $80M Foundation at regional scale per the deck's own framing.

## The headline

Conservation First is the strongest scenario by both survival rate and full-objective achievement. Even Conservation meets all six objectives only about 47 percent of the time. About half the time the deal works on the partnership's stated terms. About half the time it does not. That's worth planning around.

Two structural protections would move any chosen scenario toward better odds: closing-condition entitlement protection (investor capital held in escrow until the IRS POF determination clears, with zoning rezone secured before lot pricing is committed), and Foundation yield floor or larger per-lot contribution sizing. These are not optional refinements. They are the difference between a deal that works half the time and one that works most of the time.

## What's in the hub

**The main content.** Memo, Model, Simulator.

The **Memo** is the full analysis. Three sections: the conceptual frame, eight open questions worth the partnership's attention, and what the analysis shows across the four scenarios plus Monte Carlo findings. 8 to 10 minutes to read.

The **Model** is the parametric financial engine that produced the deterministic scenario numbers. Editable inputs, live outputs. Useful for testing your own assumptions. The full Excel workbook is also available for download.

The **Simulator** is the Monte Carlo layer that produced the survival and objective rates. Three modes: named scenarios with their distributions, distribution variants for testing assumptions, and custom configurations with design parameter sliders.

**Supporting material.** Sources, Change Log.

**Sources** lists forty-five entries across six topics covering the empirical claims in the memo. Twenty-five carry verified URLs. **Change Log** is the audit trail documenting every decision made in building the engine and the Monte Carlo.
